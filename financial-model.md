## 2. PRICING STRATEGY & MARKET POSITIONING

### 2.1 Market Context

**Israeli Summer Camp Market (2026):**
- Most Israeli "kaytanot" (day camps) charge 2,000-5,000 NIS/week
- Overnight camps for teens: 6,000-10,000 NIS for 7-10 day sessions
- Kimama (current market ceiling for premium overnight): 14,800 NIS for 13 days = 1,138 NIS/day
- Scouting/youth movement camps: 1,500-4,000 NIS for 7-14 days (heavily subsidized)
- Moshava-style camps (religious): 8,000-12,000 NIS for 2-3 week sessions

**US Camp Benchmark (converted to NIS):**
- US camps charge $800-$2,000/week = $114-$286/day
- In NIS: 416-1,044 NIS/day
- Top-tier US camps (Interlochen, Buck's Rock, Androscoggin): $2,000-$2,500/week = $286-$357/day = 1,044-1,303 NIS/day
- Average US private overnight camp: ~$1,200/week = $171/day = 625 NIS/day

**Purchasing Power Parity Adjustment:**
- Israel PPP factor vs. US: approximately 0.70-0.75 (Israeli purchasing power is 70-75% of US levels)
- US camp $171/day PPP-adjusted to Israel = 120-128 USD/day = 438-467 NIS/day
- US premium $286/day PPP-adjusted = 200-215 USD/day = 730-785 NIS/day
- The Israeli market ceiling can support a PPP-adjusted premium price given the novelty and quality gap

**Competitive Positioning:**
Our camp positions above Kimama (14,800 NIS/13 days) but below PPP-adjusted US premium camp pricing. We deliver US-level programming, staffing ratios, and facilities at a price that represents clear value versus what an Israeli family would pay to send a child to camp in the US ($8,000-$15,000 + airfare).

**Pricing Rationale:**
- Standard (14-day) at 14,900 NIS exceeds Kimama's 14,800 NIS/13 days marginally, establishing premium positioning while remaining in the "consideration zone" for Israeli families already paying 10,000-15,000 NIS for quality overnight experiences
- The 10-day session at 11,500 NIS is deliberately set at a higher per-day rate to incentivize the 14-day booking
- The 21-day session at 19,500 NIS rewards commitment with the lowest per-day rate and creates the camp's aspirational tier
- All tiers are 35-55% below what an Israeli family would pay for an equivalent US camp experience (before flights)

### 7.2 Break-Even Calculation

**Break-Even Formula:**
Break-Even Camper-Sessions = Fixed Costs / (Revenue per Camper-Session - Variable Cost per Camper-Session)

| Parameter | NIS |
|-----------|-----|
| Average revenue per camper-session (tuition net of aid + ancillary share) | 12,490 |
| Variable cost per camper-session | 5,458 |
| Contribution margin per camper-session | 7,032 |
| Annual fixed costs | 2,028,600 |
| **Break-Even Camper-Sessions** | **289** |
| **Break-Even Campers Per Session (3 sessions)** | **97** |
| **Break-Even Occupancy Rate** | **64%** |

**Interpretation:**
- At 97 campers per session (64% occupancy), the camp covers all costs
- Year 1 at 70% occupancy (105/session, 315 total) is above the raw break-even in camper count, but Year 1 has additional startup costs and lower ancillary revenue, leading to a net loss. The shortfall comes from startup-weighted fixed costs and lower-than-mature ancillary streams
- By Year 2 at 85% occupancy, the camp is comfortably profitable
- The business becomes cash-flow positive once enrollment exceeds approximately 110 campers per session with mature ancillary revenue
