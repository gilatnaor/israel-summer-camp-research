# US Camp Partnership Strategy

**Prepared: April 2026**

---

## The Credibility Thesis

US camp industry has 150+ years of brand equity. Israeli parents (especially Anglo-Israeli, upper-middle-class) are aware of the American model and aspire to it. A US partnership compresses trust-building from 5-7 years to 1-2 years.

**Target audiences who respond to US credibility:**
- Anglo-Israeli market (250K-350K Americans/Canadians in Israel)
- Upper-middle-class secular Israelis with cultural awareness
- Diaspora families looking for Israel summer programs

---

## 6 Partnership Models Compared

### Model 1: Full Franchise

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What** | License US camp brand name, curriculum, operating model |
| **Cost** | $50-150K initial + 5-10% of gross revenue ongoing |
| **Control** | LOW — franchisor dictates programming, safety, branding |
| **Credibility** | VERY HIGH — instant recognition |
| **Risk** | Loss of autonomy; 6-13% royalties on thin margins; cultural mismatch |
| **Timeline** | 18-30 months |
| **Key issue** | No major US camp has done a true international franchise |

### Model 2: Network Affiliation (ACA, FJC)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What** | Join US camp network for standards, training, quality seal |
| **Cost** | $5-15K/year |
| **Control** | HIGH — your brand, your programs, meet their standards |
| **Credibility** | MODERATE — "ACA Accredited" recognized by Anglos, not most Israelis |
| **Risk** | Limited marketing value in Israel; no camper pipeline |
| **Timeline** | 12-18 months |
| **Key org** | FJC Incubator ($500K-$1M seed + consulting for new camps) |

### Model 3: Staff Exchange

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What** | Share staff with specific US camp — their counselors come to Israel, yours go to US |
| **Cost** | $35-65K/year (flights, visas, housing) |
| **Control** | VERY HIGH — partnership of equals |
| **Credibility** | MODERATE-HIGH — "trained at [US Camp]" is marketable |
| **Risk** | Visa complications; cultural integration; relationship dependency |
| **Timeline** | 6-12 months (fastest model) |

### Model 4: Consulting/Advisory

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What** | Hire US camp professionals to design program, train staff |
| **Cost** | $30-250K depending on engagement depth |
| **Control** | VERY HIGH — you own everything |
| **Credibility** | LOW-MODERATE — "designed by [Name]" has limited consumer weight |
| **Risk** | Knowledge transfer gap; no ongoing brand halo |
| **Timeline** | 12-18 months |

### Model 5: Co-Branding (RECOMMENDED SWEET SPOT)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What** | "Camp Galil, a [US Network] affiliated camp" — your brand + their credibility |
| **Cost** | $20-80K/year (licensing fee + small revenue share 2-5%) |
| **Control** | HIGH — you run day-to-day, they set quality standards |
| **Credibility** | HIGH — US name communicates quality; marketing synergies |
| **Risk** | Reputation dependency; asymmetric partnership |
| **Timeline** | 12-24 months |

### Model 6: Acquire Existing Israeli Camp

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What** | Buy/partner with existing Israeli camp, upgrade with US operations |
| **Cost** | $50K-2M acquisition + $100-500K upgrades |
| **Control** | HIGH (if acquire) to MODERATE (if JV) |
| **Credibility** | LOW-MODERATE — inherits existing reputation |
| **Risk** | Inheriting problems; cultural overhaul resistance |
| **Timeline** | 6-18 months |

## US Organizations to Approach (Ranked by Fit)

### Tier 1: Most Likely Interested

1. **Foundation for Jewish Camp (FJC)** — umbrella org, grants, consulting, Incubator program ($500K-$1M seed). Non-denominational. Best first call.
2. **Camp Ramah / National Ramah Commission** — strongest brand in Jewish camping. Already has Israel programs. Conservative movement alignment required.
3. **URJ (Reform) Camps** — 15 camps, strong Israel connection via Heller High. Reform is minority in Israel but has established institutions.
4. **Young Judaea** — explicitly Zionist, Year Course in Israel, natural bridge.
5. **JCC Camps** — non-denominational (best fit for Israeli market), but locally operated — need specific JCC partner.

### Tier 2: Worth Exploring

6. NCSY (Orthodox Union) — already has Israel summer programs
7. Independent premium camps (Timber Lake, IHC, Brant Lake) — less likely formal partnership, possible advisory/investment
8. Outward Bound — international operations, secular appeal
9. YMCA — operates globally, possible Israel camp model

### Tier 3: Israeli Partners

10. Kibbutz movement — facilities + authentic setting (Kfar Blum, Hagoshrim, Lavi)
11. WIZO — youth village facilities
12. Tovanot Ba'Chinuch — Israeli educational credibility

## The "Extension Campus" Model (Most Innovative)

**Concept:** Israeli camp as summer extension of American camp.

- US camp runs 4-8 weeks in America (Jun-Aug)
- Israel camp runs 2-3 week session
- American campers optionally attend Israel session
- Israeli campers optionally attend US camp
- Shared: branding, color war teams, songs, traditions, CampMinder database, some staff

**Why it's compelling:**
- Built-in demand from US camp families
- Shared staff/overhead
- Unique USP — no competitor offers integrated US-Israel experience
- Foundations will fund it (Schusterman, Jim Joseph, FJC)
- Creates natural Israel-Diaspora youth pipeline

**Financial model:**
- US camp gets 10-20% of Israel session revenue
- You get referral fees for Israeli campers going to US
- Target: 80-120 campers per Israel session = $250-500K revenue

**Best US camps to approach:**
- Camp Ramah New England
- Camp Young Judaea Sprout Lake
- Eden Village Camp
- Camp Tawonga
- URJ Camp George

## Credibility WITHOUT a Partnership

If you go independent, build credibility through:

1. **Hire US camp veterans** — Former director of a known US camp as your Director ($50-80K seasonal). Most impactful single move.
2. **ACA standards alignment** — Meet ACA's ~300 standards even without formal accreditation.
3. **US tech platforms** — CampMinder + Waldo + CampDoc = $15-40K/yr but signals "we operate like a top US camp."
4. **Advisory board** — 5-8 US camp luminaries, pro bono or $1-5K/yr honorarium each.
5. **Visiting experts** — 2-3 US camp pros visit your camp each summer, 3-5 days each. $10-15K/yr total.
6. **Conference presence** — Present at ACA National, FJC Leaders Assembly.
7. **Thought leadership** — Write for Camping Magazine, get profiled in Jewish media.
8. **Endorsement videos** — Fly out US camp leaders, film testimonials.

## Recommended Phased Approach

| Phase | Timeline | Actions | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1: Foundation** | Months 1-6 | Hire US consultant. Begin ACA alignment. Build advisory board. Adopt US tech platforms. | $100-175K |
| **2: Partnership** | Months 6-12 | Approach 3-5 US camps for Extension Campus/co-brand. Engage FJC. Negotiate staff exchange. | $20-50K |
| **3: Pilot** | Months 12-18 | Run pilot 2-week session, 40-80 campers. Include 10-15 US staff + 5-10 US campers. | Operating costs |
| **4: Scale** | Months 18-30 | Deepen partnership. Expand to 2-3 sessions. Begin Extension Campus. Pursue ACA accreditation. | $30-80K/yr ongoing |

**Total partnership investment:** $155-260K (Year 1), $115-205K (Year 2), $118-195K (Year 3).

These costs are IN ADDITION to core camp operating costs.
